## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are:** Developers, productivity enthusiasts, and accessibility-minded readers who want to listen to articles, documents, and long-form text hands-free — while commuting, exercising, or resting their eyes.

**Their context:** Using the app on iPhone, often with one hand. They share articles from Safari or Chrome to the app, or paste URLs directly. They expect the app to behave like a polished podcast client — reliable playback controls, a calm interface that stays out of the way, and zero server dependency.

**The job to be done:** "Take this article I found and read it to me aloud, on-device, with no friction." The secondary job: "Let me replay things I've listened to before, or pick up where I left off."

---

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm, capable, quiet.

- **Warm** — The palette is cream and burnt orange, not clinical white or techy blue. It feels like a good reading lamp, not a flashlight.
- **Capable** — On-device CoreML inference is a genuine technical achievement. The UI acknowledges this without showing off — power is implied, not announced.
- **Quiet** — No busy animations, no aggressive colour, no notification guilt. The app recedes so the content can be the focus.

**Emotional goal:** Calm confidence. When the user taps Generate, they should feel like it will work — and it does.

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Clean, warm, minimal. Not sterile. Materiality matters — frosted cards floating on a warm cream surface, subtle shadows, gentle gradients.

**Theme:** White/cream background with burnt orange highlights.
- Background: warm cream gradient `#F9F6F0 → #F2ECE0`
- Primary accent: burnt orange `#CC5914`
- Secondary accent: warm amber `#F29919`
- Text (primary): near-black `rgb(10%, 8%, 6%)` — warm, not cold grey
- Text (muted): same, 45% opacity
- Surfaces: `.ultraThinMaterial` — light frosted glass on cream
- Borders: `black.opacity(0.08)` — barely-there definition
- Danger: red (unchanged — universally understood)

**Reference aesthetic:** The feel of a good note-taking or reading app — Bear, Reeder, Instapaper. Clean, typographic, purposeful. Not a synthesiser UI, not a dark-mode gaming app.

**Anti-references:**
- Dark OLED purple / neon teal AI aesthetic (where the app came from)
- Cluttered control panels with too many dials visible at once
- Generic iOS default blue tint with no personality

---

### Design Principles

1. **Warm, not clinical.** Every colour choice, shadow weight, and spacing decision should lean toward comfort over precision. Cream over white. Burnt orange over electric teal. Soft shadows over hard edges.

2. **Content over chrome.** The article text and the play controls are the product. UI elements should be quiet until needed — cards recede, buttons don't demand attention, the mini player is compact.

3. **Consistency through the system.** `GlassComponents.swift` is the single source of truth. Never hardcode a colour or style outside it. `glassText` and `glassTextMuted` are the only text colours. `glassAccent` and `glassAccent2` are the only accent colours.

4. **Respectful interaction.** No surprise colour shifts, no jarring transitions. Haptic feedback is medium — present enough to feel real, quiet enough not to startle. Animations are brief (200–300ms) and spring-based.

5. **Accessible by default.** Text colours achieve WCAG AA contrast on cream surfaces. Touch targets are at least 44×44 pt. Dynamic Type is supported — text sizes scale, cards adapt. Reduce Motion is respected.

### Token Reference

```swift
// Backgrounds
Color.glassDeepIndigo   // cream lightest: rgb(0.99, 0.97, 0.94)
Color.glassDeepPurple   // cream mid:     rgb(0.97, 0.94, 0.89)
Color.glassDeepBlue     // cream darkest: rgb(0.94, 0.91, 0.85)

// Accents
Color.glassAccent       // burnt orange:  rgb(0.80, 0.35, 0.08) ≈ #CC5914
Color.glassAccent2      // warm amber:    rgb(0.95, 0.60, 0.10) ≈ #F29919

// Text
Color.glassText         // near-black:    rgb(0.10, 0.08, 0.06)
Color.glassTextMuted    // near-black 45%

// Surfaces
Color.glassSurface      // black.opacity(0.03)
Color.glassBorder       // black.opacity(0.08)
Color.glassDanger       // red (unchanged)
```
